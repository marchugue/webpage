Deforestation

Deforestation is the large-scale removal of trees from forests, which leads to loss of biodiversity and disruption of ecosystems.

Causes: Deforestation is mainly caused by agriculture, logging, urban expansion, and wildfires.

Effects: The consequences include soil erosion, climate change, loss of animal habitats, flooding, and reduction of oxygen production.

Solutions: To reduce deforestation, we can plant more trees, enforce stricter logging regulations, promote sustainable agriculture, and protect existing forests.
