Ice Melting

Ice melting is the rapid loss of glaciers and polar ice due to global warming, which affects sea levels and climate patterns.

Causes: Ice melting is caused by global warming, greenhouse gas emissions, deforestation, and industrial pollution.

Effects: It results in rising sea levels, flooding, loss of polar habitats, temperature changes, and disrupted ecosystems.

Solutions: To slow ice melting, we can reduce carbon emissions, use renewable energy, protect forests, and promote climate awareness.
